Analysis

The week exposed the Suns' depth fragility in stark terms. With four rotation players simultaneously unavailable, Phoenix mustered just 77 points against Portland — a team that allowed 157 to Denver the night before. The 80-point defensive swing Portland achieved illustrates how thin the margin is when Phoenix's starters aren't available.

The silver lining: Thursday's 113-110 win over the Lakers with Booker back was a statement. The Lakers are 36-24 and one of the West's most complete teams. Phoenix's ability to close out that game without Brooks or Allen shows the core's resilience when Booker is healthy. His health remains the franchise's single most important variable for the next 6 weeks.

Upcoming window: The next 10 days — Sacramento, Chicago, New Orleans, Charlotte — represent the softest stretch remaining on the schedule. A 3-1 or 4-0 run here locks Phoenix into a firm playoff position before the brutal back half of March arrives.

The post-deadline landscape is now set. The March 1 playoff eligibility waiver deadline has passed — rosters are effectively locked for the stretch run. Any player not on a roster as of yesterday cannot participate in the 2026 playoffs, which closes out the waiver wire as a competitive tool.

The apron-related maneuvering that dominated the February 5 trade deadline continues to ripple through league business. Multiple teams — most notably the Bucks, Pistons, and Warriors — made financial decisions at the deadline that prioritized staying under the luxury tax over competitive upgrades. Adam Silver has been publicly non-committal about revisiting the apron system before the next CBA negotiation in 2029, but The Ringer and ESPN both noted this deadline produced the fewest "competitive" moves in recent memory despite strong league revenue.

The broadcast landscape is noteworthy for ownership. NBC's return to NBA coverage has been strong in the ratings, with the February 28 Lakers-Warriors game on ABC drawing strong viewership despite the teams' middle-of-the-pack records. Amazon Prime's Friday night doubleheaders have settled into a consistent audience. The league's new media deal appears to be performing above internal projections — positive for all owners on the revenue distribution front.
